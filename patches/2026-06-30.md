## GHSA-v9gv-xp36-jgj8 · rabbit_common

2026-06-30 · Erlang · **MODERATE** 5.5 · Pattern: `WEAK_CRYPTO→RANDOM`

### Root cause

The system used the Erlang node's cookie as a seed for credential obfuscation. While the cookie itself is intended to be secret, its generation or default values in certain deployment scenarios could be predictable or easily guessable, especially in containerized environments where the cookie might be derived from static values or not properly randomized.

### Impact

An attacker who can predict or guess the Erlang cookie could potentially decrypt obfuscated credentials used by the Shovel and Federation plugins, leading to unauthorized access to external systems or other RabbitMQ instances.

### Fix summary

The patch modifies the credential obfuscation mechanism. It removes the initial setting of the obfuscation secret during prelaunch configuration and instead sets it during distributed setup. Crucially, it introduces a fallback secret (`credentials_obfuscation_fallback_secret`) which defaults to `"nocookie"` if the Erlang cookie is not available or explicitly set, ensuring a more robust secret is used.
